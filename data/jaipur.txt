JAIPUR - THE PINK CITY

OVERVIEW
Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is known as the Pink City due to the distinctive color of its buildings. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, it's part of India's famous Golden Triangle tourist circuit along with Delhi and Agra.

FAMOUS PLACES

Amber Fort (Amer Fort)
Located 11km from Jaipur, this majestic fort is built with red sandstone and marble. You can reach the fort by elephant ride, jeep, or on foot. Opening hours: 8 AM to 5:30 PM. Entry fee: Indians ₹100, Foreigners ₹500. Must-see: Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), Diwan-i-Aam, Ganesh Pol. Best time to visit: Early morning to avoid crowds and heat.

City Palace
A stunning blend of Rajasthani and Mughal architecture, still home to Jaipur's royal family. The complex includes museums displaying royal costumes, weapons, and artifacts. Opening hours: 9:30 AM to 5 PM. Entry fee: ₹200-700 depending on areas accessed. Don't miss: Mubarak Mahal, Chandra Mahal, and the royal armory.

Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds)
Iconic five-story pink sandstone structure with 953 small windows (jharokhas). Built in 1799 for royal women to observe street festivals without being seen. Opening hours: 9 AM to 5 PM. Entry fee: Indians ₹50, Foreigners ₹200. Best for photography from outside; interior visit is quick.

Jantar Mantar
UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring world's largest stone sundial. Collection of 19 astronomical instruments built in 1734. Opening hours: 9 AM to 5 PM. Entry fee: Indians ₹50, Foreigners ₹200. Fascinating for science and history enthusiasts.

Nahargarh Fort
Perched on Aravalli Hills, offers panoramic views of Jaipur city. Great for sunset views. Opening hours: 10 AM to 5:30 PM. Entry fee: Indians ₹50, Foreigners ₹200. Has a restaurant with city views.

Jaigarh Fort
Known as "Fort of Victory," houses the world's largest cannon on wheels (Jaivana). Opening hours: 9 AM to 5 PM. Entry fee: Indians ₹35, Foreigners ₹85. Less crowded than Amber Fort, offers great views.

FAMOUS FOOD

Dal Baati Churma
The signature Rajasthani dish. Baati are hard wheat rolls baked in traditional clay oven, served with dal (lentils) and churma (sweet crushed wheat). Must-try at: Chokhi Dhani (ethnic village resort), Laxmi Misthan Bhandar (LMB), Spice Court.

Ghewar
Traditional Rajasthani sweet, disc-shaped, made from flour and soaked in sugar syrup. Especially popular during festivals. Best places: LMB, Rawat Mishtan Bhandar.

Pyaaz Kachori
Deep-fried pastry filled with spiced onion mixture. Popular breakfast item. Best at: Rawat Mishtan Bhandar, Samrat Restaurant.

Laal Maas
Spicy red meat curry made with mutton and fiery red chilies. For non-vegetarians who love spicy food. Try at: Handi Restaurant, 1135 AD (in Amber Fort).

Mirchi Vada
Large green chilies stuffed with spiced potato filling, coated in gram flour batter and deep-fried. Street food favorite at: Rawat, local street vendors in Johari Bazaar.

CULTURE AND CUSTOMS

Dress Code
Jaipur is conservative. Women should dress modestly - cover shoulders and knees, especially when visiting temples and religious sites. Men should avoid shorts at religious places.

Temple Etiquette
Remove shoes before entering temples. Photography may be restricted inside. Leather items (belts, wallets) not allowed in some Jain temples.

Bargaining
Expected and necessary in local markets. Start at 50% of quoted price. Fixed-price shops display boards saying "Fixed Price Shop."

Festival Culture
Major festivals: Gangaur (March-April), Teej (July-August), Diwali (October-November). During festivals, expect crowds and some shop closures but spectacular decorations and celebrations.

Language
Hindi and Rajasthani are local languages. English is widely understood in tourist areas. Learn basic Hindi phrases: "Namaste" (Hello), "Dhanyavaad" (Thank you), "Kitna hai?" (How much?).

SHOPPING

Johari Bazaar
Famous for jewelry - precious stones, kundan, meenakari work. Bargain hard. Shop during day time for safety.

Bapu Bazaar
Textiles, mojari (traditional footwear), handicrafts, souvenirs. Good for budget shopping. Closed on Tuesdays.

Tripolia Bazaar
Lac bangles, brass items, traditional Rajasthani items. Vibrant and colorful market experience.

What to Buy:
- Blue pottery (unique to Jaipur)
- Block-printed textiles
- Precious and semi-precious stones
- Mojari (embroidered shoes)
- Puppets
- Miniature paintings
- Silver jewelry

Avoid buying expensive items without proper certification, especially gems and jewelry.

SPECIALTY AND HISTORY

Pink City Heritage
All buildings in old city are painted terracotta pink. This tradition started in 1876 when Maharaja Ram Singh painted the city pink (color of hospitality) to welcome Prince of Wales. The pink color is maintained by law even today.

UNESCO World Heritage
Jaipur's walled city was designated UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2019, recognized as an exceptional example of town planning.

Astronomical Legacy
Sawai Jai Singh II, the founder, was a keen astronomer. He built five Jantar Mantars across India, with Jaipur's being the largest and best preserved.

Architectural Planning
Jaipur is one of India's first planned cities, designed according to Vastu Shastra (Indian architectural science) and Shilpa Shastra.

Royal Heritage
Jaipur's royal family still resides in part of City Palace. They're custodians of cultural heritage and often participate in festivals.

PRACTICAL TIPS

Best Time to Visit: October to March (pleasant weather). Avoid May-June (extremely hot, 40-45°C).

Getting Around: Auto-rickshaws (use meter or agree on fare first), app-based cabs (Uber, Ola), local buses, cycle-rickshaws for short distances.

Where to Stay: Budget options in Bani Park area, Mid-range near MI Road, Luxury in C-Scheme or heritage hotels.

Safety: Generally safe, but avoid isolated areas after dark. Watch belongings in crowded markets. Use official guides at monuments.

Money: Carry cash for markets and street food. ATMs widely available. Credit cards accepted in hotels and upscale restaurants.

Duration: 2-3 days adequate to cover major sights. Add a day for leisurely shopping and exploring.
