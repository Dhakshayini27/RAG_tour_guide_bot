Varanasi and Prayagraj Complete Travel Guide
1 Overview and History
Varanasi also known as Kashi or Banaras is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and is considered the spiritual capital of India It is deeply rooted in Hindu traditions and rituals especially along the sacred river Ganga where pilgrims perform daily rites prayers and cremation ceremonies believed to lead to moksha or liberation
Prayagraj formerly known as Allahabad is famed for the Triveni Sangam the confluence of the Ganga Yamuna and the mythical Saraswati making it one of Hinduism most sacred pilgrimage sites It hosts world famous religious events like the Kumbh Mela and the annual Magh Mela drawing millions of devotees
2 Best Time to Visit and Climate
Varanasi
October to March is the best time to visit with pleasant weather and festival season including Dev Deepawali and Ganga Mahotsav
April to June are hot summer months with temperatures often above 40 degrees Celsius
July to September is monsoon season with moderate to heavy rains greener landscapes but some limitations for river activities
Prayagraj
October to March offers ideal weather with cool and pleasant temperatures ranging from 10 degrees Celsius to 33 degrees Celsius which is best for sightseeing and river activities
Summer is hot and dry and temperatures can exceed 40 degrees Celsius
Monsoon brings rain lush surroundings and possible disruptions on ghats

Prayagraj

4 Top Historical and Spiritual Places
Varanasi Must Visit Sites
Kashi Vishwanath Temple one of the twelve Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva
Assi Ghat famous for morning aarti and cultural ambience
Annapurna Devi Mandir eighteenth century temple dedicated to the goddess of nourishment
Kaal Bhairav Mandir ancient temple dedicated to Bhairava a form of Shiva
Mrityunjay Mahadev Mandir also known as Ravaneshwar a historic temple with cultural importance
Ratneshwar Mahadev Temple known as the Leaning Temple
Sarnath nearby Buddhist pilgrimage site where Buddha gave his first sermon
Ramnagar Fort and Museum eighteenth century fort located across the Ganga
Prayagraj Must Visit Sites
Triveni Sangam sacred confluence of three rivers
Bade Hanuman Mandir famous reclining Hanuman idol near Sangam
Alopi Devi Temple one of the fifty one Shakti Peethas with legendary spiritual significance
Allahabad Fort historic fort built by Akbar in 1583
Chandra Shekhar Azad Park large historic urban park with heritage memorials
Saraswati Ghat scenic river ghat with evening lamp ceremonies
Museums and cultural spots including the Allahabad Museum and colonial era buildings
