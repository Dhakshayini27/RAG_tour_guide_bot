BENGALURU (BANGALORE) - COMPLETE TRAVEL GUIDEOVERVIEW AND HISTORYBengaluru, the capital of Karnataka, is known as the Silicon Valley of India due to its thriving IT industry. Blending modern infrastructure with historic landmarks and lush greenery, the city is also called the Garden City of India.Historical Background:- Founded in 1537 by Kempe Gowda I- Ruled by the Wodeyars of Mysore, later by Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan- Became a major administrative and military center under British rule- Post-independence, it transformed into India's leading technology and startup hubToday, Bengaluru is known for its pleasant climate, cosmopolitan culture, nightlife, and tech parks.BEST TIME TO VISIT AND CLIMATEClimate Overview:Bengaluru has a moderate tropical savanna climate with pleasant weather most of the year.Season: Winter (November to February)Temperature Range: 15 to 28 degrees CelsiusTravel Suitability: Best time to visitSeason: Summer (March to May)Temperature Range: 20 to 35 degrees CelsiusTravel Suitability: Warm but manageableSeason: Monsoon (June to September)Temperature Range: 19 to 29 degrees CelsiusTravel Suitability: Green and scenicBest Time to Visit: October to February - Cool weather, ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.HOW TO REACHBy Air:- Kempegowda International Airport (BLR)- Well-connected to major domestic and international citiesBy Train:Major railway stations:- KSR Bengaluru City Junction- Yesvantpur Junction- Bengaluru CantonmentBy Road:- Well-connected via NH44, NH75, NH48- Frequent buses from Chennai, Hyderabad, Mysuru, Coimbatore, and other citiesLocal Transport:- Namma Metro (efficient and modern)- BMTC buses (extensive network)- Auto-rickshaws, taxis (Ola, Uber)- Bike rentalsTOP TOURIST ATTRACTIONSGardens and Parks:Lalbagh Botanical Garden:- 240-acre garden with glass house and rare plants- Founded in 1760 by Hyder Ali- Famous flower shows during Republic Day and Independence Day- Opening hours: 6 AM to 7 PM- Entry fee: Rs. 10 for adultsCubbon Park:- Green oasis in city center- 300 acres of lush greenery- Popular for morning walks and jogging- Free entryBannerghatta Biological Park:- Safari and zoo experience- Butterfly park- Located 22 km from city center- Opening hours: 9:30 AM to 5 PM- Entry fee: Rs. 100 (varies by section)Historical and Cultural Sites:Bangalore Palace:- Tudor-style palace inspired by Windsor Castle- Built in 1878- Audio guide available- Opening hours: 10 AM to 5:30 PM- Entry fee: Rs. 280 for IndiansTipu Sultan's Summer Palace:- Indo-Islamic architecture- Built in 1791- Beautiful teakwood pillars- Entry fee: Rs. 20 for IndiansVidhana Soudha:- Karnataka's legislative building- Neo-Dravidian architecture- Beautiful when lit up in the evening- Not open for tourists inside, only exterior viewingKempegowda Tower:- Historic watchtower- Built by city's founder- Located in LalbaghTemples and Spiritual Places:ISKCON Temple (Rajajinagar):- Major Krishna temple- Large complex with cultural center- Daily rituals and festivals- Opening hours: 7:15 AM to 1 PM, 4 PM to 8:30 PM- Free entryBull Temple (Dodda Basavana Gudi):- Famous for giant Nandi statue (16 feet tall)- Monolithic structure carved from single rock- Annual groundnut fair- Always open, no entry feeGavi Gangadhareshwara Temple:- Cave temple dedicated to Shiva- Unique astronomical significance- Sun rays pass through temple during Makar Sankranti- Best visited during festival timeShivoham Shiva Temple:- Large 65-foot Shiva statue- Modern temple complex- Peaceful atmosphereMuseums and Art:Visvesvaraya Industrial and Technological Museum:- Science museum with interactive exhibits- Great for children- Entry fee: Rs. 50National Gallery of Modern Art:- Contemporary Indian art- Regular exhibitions- Free entryHAL Aerospace Museum:- Aircraft and helicopter displays- India's aviation history- Entry fee: Rs. 50FOOD AND LOCAL CUISINEBengaluru offers a mix of traditional Karnataka cuisine and global food culture.Must-Try Local Dishes:Bisi Bele Bath:- Spiced rice and lentil dish- Comfort food staple- Available everywhereMysore Pak:- Traditional sweet made from gram flour, ghee, and sugar- Originally from Mysore- Popular souvenirRagi Mudde:- Finger millet balls served with sambar- Healthy traditional dish- Try at local Karnataka restaurantsThatte Idli:- Large, flat idlis- Served with chutney and sambar- Breakfast specialtyMaddur Vada:- Crispy onion fritters- Originally from Maddur town- Popular snackFilter Coffee:- Traditional South Indian coffee- Strong and aromatic- Available at every cornerStreet Food and Popular Items:- Masala Dosa (crispy crepe with potato filling)- Vada Pav (spiced potato fritter in bread)- Chaat varieties (sweet and savory snacks)- Shawarma and rolls- Pani puri- Gobi ManchurianFAMOUS RESTAURANTS AND CAFESTraditional Karnataka Cuisine:MTR (Mavalli Tiffin Rooms):- Iconic restaurant since 1924- Famous for traditional breakfast- Try their rava idli- Multiple locationsVidyarthi Bhavan:- Famous for crispy dosas- Been serving since 1943- Always crowded, worth the waitCTR (Central Tiffin Room):- Traditional breakfast spot- Benne dosa (butter dosa) is legendary- In Malleshwaram areaFine Dining:Karavalli (The Gateway Hotel):- Coastal cuisine specialist- Authentic seafood- Premium dining experienceRim Naam (The Oberoi):- Thai cuisine- Elegant setting- Outdoor seating availableOlive Beach:- Mediterranean cuisine- Trendy atmosphere- Good for brunchCafes and Breweries:Toit Brewpub:- Craft beer on tap- European food- Very popular, arrive earlyArbor Brewing Company:- American-style brewpub- Good pizzas- Outdoor seatingThird Wave Coffee:- Specialty coffee chain- Local favorite- Multiple locationsThe Hole in the Wall Cafe:- Cozy cafe- Good breakfast and coffee- Instagram-worthyNote: Bengaluru is known as the Pub Capital of India with numerous breweries and pubs.SHOPPING SUGGESTIONSStreet and Budget Markets:Commercial Street:- Clothes, accessories, jewelry- 1.5 km shopping stretch- Bargaining expectedChickpet use bottled water- Auto-rickshaws should use meters or agree on fare beforehand- Download Ola or Uber apps for convenient taxi service- Keep some cash handy as not all places accept cardsSafety:- Generally safe city for tourists- Avoid isolated areas late at night- Keep belongings secure in crowded markets- Use registered taxis or app-based cabsLanguage:- Kannada is local language- English is widely spoken- Hindi is understood in many areas- Most signboards are in English and KannadaQUICK FACTSState: KarnatakaLanguage: Kannada (English widely spoken)Currency: Indian Rupee (INR)Time Zone: IST (UTC +5:30)Area Code: +91-80Population: Approximately 12 million (metro area)Elevation: 920 meters (3,020 feet) above sea levelAirport: Kempegowda International Airport (BLR)Pin Code: 560001 (central area, varies by location)Known For:- IT industry and tech parks- Pleasant climate year-round- Gardens and green spaces- Nightlife and pub culture- Startup ecosystem- Cosmopolitan cultureBest Months to Visit: October to FebruaryAverage Annual Rainfall: 970 mmLiteracy Rate: 88 percent (one of highest in India)NEARBY WEEKEND GETAWAYSNandi Hills (60 km):- Hill station with sunrise views- Ancient Nandi temple- Trekking and cycling trails- Drive time: 1.5 hoursMysuru (145 km):- Mysore Palace- Chamundi Hills temple- Brindavan Gardens- Drive time: 3 hoursCoorg (265 km):- Coffee plantations- Abbey Falls- Wildlife sanctuaries- Drive time: 5-6 hoursChikmagalur (245 km):- Coffee estates- Mullayanagiri peak (highest in Karnataka)- Baba Budangiri hills- Drive time: 4-5 hoursHampi (340 km):- UNESCO World Heritage Site- Ancient ruins and temples- Historical monuments- Drive time: 6-7 hoursKabini (220 km):- Wildlife sanctuary- Boat safaris- Nature resort stays- Drive time: 4 hours
